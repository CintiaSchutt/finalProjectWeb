Carnival

Carnival is one of the events that most attract visitors to the State of Rio de Janeiro.

The greatest moment of this party usually celebrated in February, is the parade at Passarela Professor Darcy Ribeiro, the Sambódromo of Marquês de Sapucaí.

The Sambódromo was designed by the architecture Oscar Niemeyer and was inaugurated in 1984. For four consecutive days of Carnival, more than twelve samba schools parade with their floats, tireless dancers, lively percussion and luxurious and colourful costumes, cheering the revellers all through the night.

Known as the largest party on the planet, the Carnival in Rio de Janeiro also gathers people in other parts of the city.

This gathering is called street blocks. Without the grandiosity of Sapucaí parade, the revellers in these blocks dance and have fun in the streets, wearing costumes or not.

During the preparation for the Carnival, it is possible to take part on cheerful samba school rehearsals.
